Environmental, Social & Governance Analysis

ESG Integration Process

Our ESG integration goes beyond simple screening to comprehensive analysis of how environmental, social, and governance factors impact long-term investment returns and risk profiles.

Our ESG Framework

Environmental Factors

  • Climate Action: Carbon reduction targets and renewable energy transition
  • Resource Management: Water usage, waste reduction, sustainable sourcing
  • Environmental Risk: Regulatory compliance, pollution control, biodiversity impact
  • Innovation: Investment in clean technology and sustainable solutions

Social Factors

  • Employee Relations: Fair wages, benefits, diversity and inclusion
  • Community Impact: Local economic development, social programs
  • Product Safety: Quality control, consumer protection, public health impact
  • Supply Chain: Ethical sourcing, labor standards, human rights

Governance Factors

  • Board Structure: Independence, diversity, oversight effectiveness
  • Executive Compensation: Alignment with long-term performance
  • Transparency: Financial reporting quality, stakeholder communication
  • Risk Management: Internal controls, compliance, ethical leadership

Integration Methodology

1. Data Collection

We gather ESG data from multiple sources: - Third-party providers: MSCI, Sustainalytics, Bloomberg ESG - Company disclosures: Sustainability reports, SEC filings - Independent research: NGO reports, academic studies - Direct engagement: Management interviews, site visits

2. Materiality Assessment

Not all ESG factors are equally important for every industry: - Sector-specific risks: Climate impact varies by industry - Regulatory environment: Different compliance requirements - Stakeholder priorities: Customer, employee, community expectations - Competitive dynamics: ESG as differentiation or table stakes

3. Quantitative Analysis

ESG factors are integrated into financial models: - Risk adjustment: Higher discount rates for ESG risks - Growth projections: ESG leaders often have better growth prospects - Multiple expansion: ESG premium in market valuations - Scenario analysis: Stress testing for ESG-related disruptions

Screening Policy

Our screening process aligns with goodness and avoids preventable harm to living things through systematic exclusion criteria:

Core Philosophy

We view ethical screening as a primary risk management tool, systematically excluding investments that pose “preventable harm as investment risk” including regulatory sanctions, reputational damage, stranded assets, operational disruptions, and reduced capital market access.

Product-Based Exclusions

  1. Harm to Living Beings: Companies whose products directly harm people or animals
  2. Weapons and Military: Defense contractors and weapons manufacturers
  3. Addictive Products: Tobacco, gambling, and other dependency-creating industries
  4. Fossil Fuels: Oil, gas, coal extraction and processing companies
  5. Extractive Industries: Mining and resource extraction with environmental impact
  6. Surveillance Capitalism: Companies monetizing personal data without consent
  7. For-profit Incarceration: Private prison and detention facility operators

Conduct-Based Exclusions

  1. Direct Harm and Rights Violations: Companies with documented human rights abuses
  2. Environmental and Climate Misconduct: Persistent environmental violations
  3. Governance and Operational Failures: Poor corporate governance and ethical lapses
  4. Financial and Social Harm: Predatory lending and exploitative business practices

Implementation Strategy

  • Growth Strategy: 100% direct implementation with high-conviction portfolios
  • Income Strategy: Flexible approach, preferring aligned managers
  • Diversification Strategy: Indirect implementation through aligned funds

Ongoing Monitoring

  • Monthly portfolio reviews using internal research (no third-party ESG rating reliance)
  • Key performance indicator: “Do portfolio companies align with goodness and avoid preventable harm to living things?”

ESG Integration in Practice

Portfolio Construction

ESG analysis influences: - Security selection: Preference for ESG leaders within sectors - Position sizing: Larger allocations to companies with improving ESG profiles - Sector allocation: Overweight sectors with positive ESG trends - Risk management: ESG factor-based hedging strategies

Active Ownership

We engage with portfolio companies on: - Climate targets: Science-based emission reduction goals - Diversity initiatives: Board and workforce diversity improvements - Supply chain ethics: Labor standards and human rights policies - Governance reforms: Executive compensation and board structure

Impact Measurement

Regular tracking of: - ESG score evolution: Improvement in company ratings over time - Carbon footprint: Portfolio-wide emission reductions - Engagement outcomes: Success rate of proxy voting and dialogue - Financial performance: ESG integration impact on returns

Case Studies

Successful ESG Integration

Examples of companies that demonstrated: - Strong ESG leadership driving outperformance - Effective risk management through ESG practices - Innovation in sustainable solutions creating competitive advantage

ESG Risk Management

Cases where ESG analysis prevented losses: - Early identification of governance risks - Climate-related business model disruption - Social license to operate challenges

Continuous Improvement

Our ESG integration process evolves through: - Research updates: Incorporating new academic findings - Data enhancement: Adding new ESG data sources and metrics - Methodology refinement: Improving quantitative integration techniques - Client feedback: Adapting to changing investor priorities
